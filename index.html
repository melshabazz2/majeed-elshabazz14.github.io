About Me

My name is Majeed El-Shabazz, an aspiring software engineer from East Flatbush, Brooklyn. Growing up in a Caribbean community played a significant role in shaping my identity, values, and work ethic. The resilience, creativity, and sense of community I witnessed daily continue to inspire how I live my life and use technology.

Prior to joining The Marcy Lab School, I worked as a security officer across homeless shelters throughout Brooklyn. That experience strengthened my discipline, accountability, and ability to remain calm under pressure. A pivotal moment for me was a conversation with a Marcy Lab alumnus who shared how the program transformed his life, motivating me to pursue a path I had always been curious about but never thought possible.

My journey has required overcoming both personal and external challenges. I made intentional decisions to remove negative influences, replace unproductive habits with disciplined routines, and prioritize my mental and educational growth. Every step I take now is an investment in my future.

Why Programming?

Technology inspires me because it solves real-world problems, creates tools people rely on daily, and offers financial independence. I enjoy building applications, contributing to games I grew up playing, and understanding how systems work behind the scenes.

What I Bring?

I bring resilience, adaptability, and leadership shaped by my upbringing in Brooklyn. I believe determination, discipline, and self-belief can overcome any obstacle.
